Tony Awards Return to the Stage


Exciting news for Broadway fans! The Tony Awards ceremony is returning to the stage this year after a hiatus due to the COVID-19 pandemic. The ceremony, which celebrates excellence in Broadway theatre, will take place on September 26, 2021, and will be broadcast on CBS.

New Shows Coming to Broadway


Several new shows are also set to open on Broadway in the coming months, including "The Music Man" starring Hugh Jackman and Sutton Foster, which is scheduled to begin previews on December 20, 2021, and officially open on February 10, 2022. Other highly anticipated productions include "Six," a modern retelling of the story of Henry VIII's six wives, and a revival of "The Phantom of the Opera."

Platt speaks out against antisemitic protests of ‘Parade’ on Broadway


The Broadway revival of the musical "Parade" about the dangers of hatred faced anti-Semitic protesters outside the theater during its first preview. The protesters harassed audience members and spread hateful rhetoric about the Jewish man whom the show is based on, reinforcing the importance of the musical's message. The musical tells the true story of Leo Frank, a Jewish man convicted of murder in 1913 due to anti-Semitic prejudice in the South. Antisemitic attacks have been on the rise in recent years, making the musical's message even more urgent.

Nonbinary Broadway Performer Opts Out of Gendered Tony Awards

The Tony Awards' gendered categories are facing renewed scrutiny following the decision of a principal performer in the new Broadway musical "& Juliet," Justin David Sullivan, to withdraw from consideration for a Tony rather than compete in a gendered category. Sullivan, who is trans nonbinary, plays May, one of Juliet's best friends in the show. The Tony Awards, like the Oscars and the Emmys, have separate acting categories for men and women, but the Grammy Awards eliminated many gendered categories as part of a consolidation in 2012, and the Obie Awards have long had nongendered categories. The Tony Awards have accepted Sullivan's position, and there are ongoing discussions about how to best address the issue of gender identity in future seasons. Several regional theater award competitions have already eliminated gender-specific awards categories.
